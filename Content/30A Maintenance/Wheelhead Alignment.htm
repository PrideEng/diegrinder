Wheelhead Alignment (A Axis)

  1. Mount indicator on wheel head spindle using 9-106.

  2. Rotate indicator until it is pointing backwards and hold it in this position.

  3. Turn key to "OFF" position (this key position holds the spindles and keeps them from rotating).

  4. Remove shot pin 19-199 from C axis rotary base and move C axis to 90°.

  5. Move A axis and Z axis in small increments until indicator is resting on the near side of tooling ball face.

  6. Move A axis in the (+) and (-) direction with the indicator on the near side of the tooling ball face. If alignment is within +/- 0.0003 inch (0.007 mm) move ahead to step 10.

  7. Loosen mounting bolts of Z axis base (6-135). Keep front left bolt tightened for pivot point.

  8. Move A axis in the (+) direction and (-) while tapping in casting until alignment is within +/- 0.0003 inch (0.007 mm).

  9. Tighten down bolts while verifying alignment.

  10. Move A axis to safe position.

  11. Move C axis to 0°.

  12. Insert shot pin 19-199 into 6-111to keep C axis at 0°.

  13. Turn key to the "MAN" position.

  14. Move X axis and Y axis in increments until the tooling ball is near center of C axis rotation pivot point.

  15. Move Z axis in increments until the wheel head spindle center line is near center of C axis rotation pivot point.

  16. Move A axis in until indicator touches face of tooling ball. Sweep indicator around tooling ball face. If alignment is within +/- 0.0003 inch (0.007 mm), skip to step 20.

  17. Loosen wheel head air bearing housing bolts and remove front dowel pin.

  18. Keep rear dowel pin in place.

  19. Sweep the tooling ball face with the indicator while tapping in housing until alignment is within +/- 0.0003 inch (0.007 mm).

  20. Tighten bolts while checking alignment, replace front dowel pin.

  21. Move A axis to safe position.

  22. Remove shot pin 19-199 from C axis rotary base.
