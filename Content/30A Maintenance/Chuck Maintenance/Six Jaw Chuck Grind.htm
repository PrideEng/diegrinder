Six Jaw Chuck Maintenance

Occasionally the work holding chuck must be ground to maintain flatness and squareness. Failure to re-grind the chuck when required will cause the ground work piece to be distorted and out of round when removed from the chuck. The face of a redraw die will check out of flat when ground in a distorted state. First the grinding wheel used for grinding the chuck must be dressed or the quality of the ground chuck will be distorted. Use the following procedures to dress the grinding wheel used for grinding the chuck.

You must first dress the Chuck Grinding Grit Wheel

See: Dressing the Chuck Grinding Wheel with the Single Point Diamond

Unlike conventional machine chucks where the jaws slide in and out, the 6-Jaw Air Actuated Chuck operates on an air diaphragm that cantilevers the six jaws out away from center when charged with air. When the air pressure is absent, the six jaws close by cantilevering toward the center, gripping the work piece. During normal operations the chuck is opened or cantilevered out with 80 lbs. (5.5 bar) of air pressure to change parts.

When the operator depresses the button to change dies the air pressure is applied and the jaws cantilever open. As soon as the operator releases the button, the chuck jaws close.

This mechanism achieves much better concentricity than would be possible with a conventional sliding jaw chuck.

A - Switch to "Grind Chuck Jaws" when grinding work holding chuck, Use "Run" for normal operation

B - Work holding chuck jaw air pressure when in "Run" mode. (80 )(5.5)

C - Work holding chuck jaw air pressure when in "Grind Chuck Jaws" mode. (50 )(3.5)

Verify the position of selector switch A is appropriate for the step you are performing in the process.

When grinding a work holding chuck made to hold 2 sizes, jaws for both sizes need to be ground.

 

The "Grind Chuck Jaws" mode allows the jaws to be held square without a die present. If the chuck is in "Run" mode and no die is present the jaws will be tilted inwards. When it is time to regrind the jaws of the chuck, the jaws must be held square without a die present. If the jaws are ground while in "Run" mode, the die will not properly seat in the jaws when the chuck is returned to service.

The C axis must be in the zero position when grinding the chuck jaws and chuck spider. The most accurate method is to move the C axis to the zero position and then pin it in position using the following process.

Turn key to "OFF" position.

Move C axis to 0°.

Insert 19-199 C Axis Locking Pin into 6-111 Lock Pin Plate.

If you are grinding both the chuck jaws and the chuck spider, it makes no difference which is ground first.

6 inch jaw grind

  1. Select "Grind Chuck Jaws" on the selector located on the gauge panel.

  2. Adjust the location of each of the six (6) chuck jaws so that there will be interference fit between the chuck jaws and the die of 0.0025” to 0.005” (0.065 mm to 0.130 mm). As an example, if you are grinding 6 inch dies, adjust the jaws to 5.995 inch diameter. Start the process by moving each opposing jaw inward toward center an equal amount.

  3. Use an indicator to check the concentricity of the jaws. Adjust each jaw to achieve concentricity within 0.0005" (0.130 mm).

  4. Mark the surface of each jaw where it contacts the die with bluing or similar marking material. This will enable the operator to know when all jaw surfaces have been ground.

  5. Mount chuck grinding wheel to the wheel head spindle.

  6. Dress the wheel if needed: Dressing the Chuck Grinding Wheel with the Single Point Diamond

  7. Turn the key to Auto mode.

Make sure the Control Power On Icon is on before proceeding to the next step.

  2. Select "Six Jaw Chuck Regrinding"

  4. Move the cursor to the    icon. Depress left mouse button until the green icon turns gray.

 

  1. Select the icon (OP3) and verify that the program "Sixjaw.npg" is selected.

  2. Depress the Cycle Start button  . Verify the Z axis is centered with the chuck.

  3. Switch the key Man mode. This will allow the operator to determine the position for "A Stroke in" and "A Stroke Out"

  4. Jog A axis in the (+) direction until the front edge of the grinding wheel resides in the relief cut of the jaw, while avoiding a collision with the chuck. Record the A position value. This will be the "A Stroke In" value to be entered into the form.

  5. Jog A axis in the (–) direction until the front edge of the wheel is approximately 0.060 inches (1.5 mm) from the outer edge of the jaw. Record the A position value. This will become the "A Stroke Out" position value to be entered into the form.

  6. Perform a manual touch off on the chuck jaw, just as you would on a die. Set Z zero.

  7. Turn the key to "Auto Mode" mode.

  8. Select "Dressing and Regrinding" from the machine screen.

  9. Select "Six Jaw Chuck Regrinding"

  10. When the Six Jaw Chuck Regrinding screen appears, enter the values for "A Stroke In" and "A Stroke Out" recorded in steps 14 and 15 by entering the values and selecting the yellow button.

  11. Select the icon (OP3) and verify that the program "Sixjaw.npg" is selected.

  12. Depress the Cycle Start button  once.

  13. The oscillation cycle will begin, and the machine will do a program stop (M00).

  2. Set the hand wheel increment switch to .00001 and the axis to Z.

  1. Turn the hand wheel one click at a time, allowing spark on the jaw to occur, continue the process until the desired amount of material has been removed from the jaws.
