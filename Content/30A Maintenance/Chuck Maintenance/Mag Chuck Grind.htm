Grinding a Magnetic Chuck

The magnetic chuck surface needs to be flat to achieve the accuracy of the grinding process. It may be necessary to periodically regrind the chuck surface. Failure to do this when required will cause the work piece to be distorted when removed from the chuck and inspected in a normal state. Use the following procedures to re- grind the magnetic chuck face.

 

  1. Move the C axis to zero degrees. Install your indicator on the quill nose with an adapter (as shown in the picture above). Indicate the face of the work holding chuck by sweeping with the quill. The work holding chuck should be set either at zero position or so that land would increase slightly in the direction of ironing.

  2. Move C axis to 90 degrees and install test bar. Indicate test bar by moving wheel head slide forward. If test bar reads zero, you may begin grinding. If run out exists, the chuck must be adjusted by grinding or shimming the rest button.

  1. Install the 4 inch grinding wheel. Remove the test bar and energize the chuck.

  2. Follow the Chuck Wheel dressing procedure for the magnetic chuck grinding wheel. See: Dressing the Chuck Grinding Wheel with the Single Point Diamond

  3. The single point diamond fixture may be left in place for subsequent dressing while regrinding the chuck.

  1. After the grinding wheel is true, move the Z axis until wheel touches the magnetic chuck. Set Z zero and back off the Z axis. Set A axis slide so that grinding wheel clears the chuck at both ends of the stroke. The work holding spindle should be run at 50 rpm and move the Z axis inward in 0.0001inch increments. The magnetic chuck should be fully energized. Do not exceed 50% speed on the grinding wheel spindle. Use coolant while grinding.

  2. Move Z axis back to zero. Observe increased torque reading on spindle Touch Off Meter as Z axis is moved in.

  1. Correct procedure allows spindle to extend and retract once for each .0001 inches (2.5 microns) that the Z axis is moved in. After feed in is complete, allow 2 additional spark out passes of the wheel head. Check chuck for clean-up. If the grinding wheel loads up during grinding, it must be redressed on the diamond.

  2. Mount the tooling ball in the chuck after the chuck grinding is complete and reset the X and Y axis zero. See: Setting X & Y Axis Zero - Tooling Ball Zero
