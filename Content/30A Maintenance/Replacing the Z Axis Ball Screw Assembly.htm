Replacing the Z Axis Ball Screw Assembly

FOLLOW ALL SAFETY GUIDELINES WHEN PERFORMING MAINTENANCE

  1. In manual mode on the control panel, bring the Z position towards the operator to its soft stop in the program.

  2. Emergency stop the system

  3. Remove the belt guard

  4. Remove the drive belt to the to the Z axis

  5. Remove the motor mount

  6. Turning the pulley by hand will move the casting closer to the operator side. NOTE: The encoder scale may come to end of travel. Do not force the pulley; it may damage the encoder scale. You may want to remove the encoder guard to better see the end of travel on the encoder scale.

  7. Remove the ball screw mount block from the end of the casting

  8. Manually rotate the pulley on the Z axis moving the mount block away from the casting. Do not unscrew the lead screw from the ball nut, the bearing balls will fall out.

  9. Manually push the Z casting closer to the operator side. Again do not force the end of travel on the encoder scale. The casting may be difficult to move at the current gibb tension.

  10. The nut retainer block may be visible at this point

  11. Remove the four bolts holding the nut retainer block

  12. Remove the ball screw assembly and install the new ball screw assembly. You will need to disassemble the old assembly to use the casting mount block. NOTE: Once the motor and belt are reassembled, Pride will need to reference the motor remotely before die grinding.

  13. If the ball screw assembly still cannot be removed, the encoder scale read head bracket may need to be unbolted. It is not the main scale. It is the mechanism underneath the encoder scale the cable attaches to. Be careful not to twist the bracket or remove the read head causing damage to the scale. The encoder guard will be removed to accomplish this task.

  14. Once the bracket is remove the Z axis can travel closer to the operator side allowing access to the nut retainer block.
