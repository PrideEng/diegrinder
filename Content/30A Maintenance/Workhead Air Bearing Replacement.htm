Workhead Air Bearing Replacement Procedure

READ MANUAL THOROUGHLY BEFORE INSTALLING OR OPERATING EQUIPMENT

FOLLOW ALL SAFETY GUIDELINES WHEN PERFORMING MAINTENANCE

  1. Throughout this process, you can use a wood block to stop the chuck from rotating.

  2. Remove the rear safety covers

  3. Remove the belt

  4. Using a hammer and flat head screwdriver, or a spanner wrench, loosen and remove the pulley nut. Be sure to secure the chuck with a wood block to stop it from rotating.

  5. Remove the Pulley

  6. Remove the chuck spider

  7. Remove bolts from center cap

  8. Use screwdrivers or a pry bar to remove center cap

  9. Remove the 8 bolts securing the chuck to the air bearing using a 5/32" hex wrench.

  10. Remove Chuck

  11. Remove Air Bearing Safety Cover

  12. Shut off air and disconnect all air lines

  13. Remove air pressure sensor and air fittings

  14. Loosen the air bearing housing bolts

  15. Tighten the set screw to "open" the housing and allow the air bearing to be removed

  16. Remove the air bearing. Do not spin or rotate the inner bearing during this process. Spinning the bearing without air could damage the bearing.

  17. Reverse this process to install the new air bearing.

    Setting X & Y Axis Zero - Tooling Ball Zero
