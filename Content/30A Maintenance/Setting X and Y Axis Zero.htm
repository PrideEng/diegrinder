Setting X & Y Axis Zero - Tooling Ball Zero

Grinder must be square prior to this procedure.

See: Workhead Alignment (Y Axis)

See: WheelHead casting alignment

See: Wheelhead Alignment (A Axis)

  1. At the opening screen select "Grinder Set Up".

  2. Turn key to Man mode.

  3. Load 6 inch tooling ball fixture (19-127) into chuck.

  4. Rotate the C Axis to 0.

  5. If the tooling ball was previously set and needs a minor adjustment, move the X and Y axis to 0 and proceed to step

  6. The center of the tooling ball should be on center with the shot pin hole. Do this using a straight edge.

  7. Using the side of a straight edge against the tooling ball fixture face, move the X axis so the left edge of the straight edge is centered on the shot pin bore.

    Note: The straight edge is against the tooling ball fixture and not the jaws.

  9. The offset from the center of the tooling ball to the top of the tooling ball fixture is .309” (7.8486mm). Once X is visually lined up, move X axis plus .309” inches (7.8486mm) to accommodate the offset

  10. Rotate C to 90°.

  11. Visually line up the center of the tooling ball to the center of the shot pin bushing perpendicular to the tooling ball fixture. Note: The centers of the ruler, tooling ball, and shot pin are all in line with each other.

  12. At this time it is important that the indicator tip be set at center height of the tooling ball

  13. The previous steps assist in achieving a rough position of the tooling ball. The next steps will help bring this closer so you can finally contact the tooling ball with the indicator for fine adjustments.

  14. At C 0° set indicator point near front and center of ball. Note: Indicator is close but not touching the tooling ball.

  15. Rotate C axis to 90°.

  16. While rotating note the gap between the indicator and tooling ball. The goal is to have an even gap the entire time the C axis is moving for 0 to 90 and 90 to 0. Adjust X or Y axis as needed to even the gap between the indicator and tooling ball.

  17. You are now ready for fine adjustments.

  18. Rotate C to 0°. Move the indicator onto the tooling ball and zero the indicator on the tooling ball. NOTE: You must use an indicator capable of .0001" or finer resolution. Using a metric indicator capable of measuring microns during fine positioning will add accuracy to this process.

  19. Rotate C to 45°. Note distance and direction indicator moved at 45°. (Example, at 45° indicator moved +.004).

  20. Rotate C to 0°. Zero the indicator.

  21. Adjust Y twice the distance in the opposite direction the indicator moved at 45°. (Example, Adjust Y -.008).

  22. Rotate C to 90°. Zero the indicator.

  23. Adjust X twice the distance in the opposite direction the indicator moved at 45° per bullet point (13). (Example, Adjust X -.008).

  24. Repeat steps 12-17 until indicator reads 0 all the way through the C axis rotation from C = 0° to C = 90°. You will be adjusting the X with C at 90° and adjusting Y with C at 0°. You will adjust both the X and Y equally with C at 45°.

  25. Continue to Jog the X and Y work head slides until the indicator does not move when rotating the C axis through 90 degrees of travel. You should be able to achieve +/- .00005 or better.

  26. You are now on center with the Y axis and exactly 0.5000 inches (12.7mm) from the face of the chuck. You are ready to set zero accordingly.

  27. Select the maintenance icon at the lower right of the screen.

  28. Select the icon at the lower left hand of the screen.

  29. Turn the key to "Auto mode"

  30. Select the X Axis by touching the gray icon on the right side of the screen. Notice that the of the X Axis resets to zero automatically after the is selected. Select "Close Active Screen". Select the Y axis by touching the gray icon . Select the icon, it will automatically reset the Y Axis to zero. Select "Close Active Screen". You have now completed the tooling ball procedure. If you are using a 6/7 inch step jaw chuck, continue on with the following steps:

  31. Load the 7 Inch Tooling Ball (9-185) in the 7 Inch jaws.

  32. Maintain the Y axis position at the zero position set in Step 15 and the C axis at 0 degrees.

  33. Select the X Axis so that you can observe the current position and move the X Axis in the (-) direction approximately 0.600 inches (15.24 mm).

  34. Place the indicator tip on the tooling ball side closest to the operator.

  35. Rotate the C axis manually through 90 degrees and observe the indicator. Move the X Axis until the indicator remains within +/- 0.0001 inches (0.0025mm).

  36. Record the Y Axis current position value. This value will be used in any 7 Inch Re-Draw Die programs window.
