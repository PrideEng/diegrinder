Safety

Proper Use for Intended Purpose

Applications

The machine is intended for the can making industry for following applications only:

  • Grinding ironing dies and re-draw dies utilized in the two-piececan making process and held by a work piece clamping device.

  • The machine is intended to grind carbide material only.

  • Dressing of the grinding wheel used in the machine by utilizing the dressing tool mounted in the machine.

 

Workpiece Holding

The work piece must be held securely in a clamping device integral to the machine. The machine is made to work with a six-jaw chuck or magnetic chuck.

Acceptable Coolant Type

The machine is designed to work with Pride Coolant mixed 20 parts water to 1 part coolant.

Pride Coolant: 19-217A

Acceptable Lubrication Type

The machine is designed to utilize oil for lubricating the ways and ball screws.

Servicing

Service of the machine should only be performed by qualified and trained maintenance personnel.

Improper Use

 

The following are improper, prohibited uses of the machine.

•    Using the machine with protective devices removed or bypassed.

•    Using the machine with safety warning labels removed or illegible.

•    Using the machine while holding the work piece or tooling by hand.

•    Using handheld wheel dressing tools.

•    Using the machinery without proper work holding.

•    Using the machine with cutting tools instead of grinding wheels.

•    Grinding without coolant.

 

User Safety

Operators of the equipment should do so wearing eye protection. Operators and maintenance personnel should read the user’s manual prior to operating the equipment. Only trained maintenance personnel should perform maintenance on the equipment and should follow control of hazardous energy procedures (Lock Out- Tag Out) to ensure maintenance can be performed safely.

Residual Risk

Hazards which cannot be eliminated or countered by design measures are residual risks. Residual risks are documented in this manual and noted by the symbols utilizing a yellow triangle.

Environmental

Handle and dispose of coolants and lubricants in accordance with local and national environmental protection and disposal requirements.

 

All fluids, filter papers and filters should be handled in accordance with local and national environmental standards.
