Grinder Installation Info

READ MANUAL THOROUGHLY BEFORE INSTALLING OR OPERATING EQUIPMENT

Unloading, Moving and Unpacking

The packaging of the equipment includes important information on the outside of the packaging as to the proper treatment during transportation, unloading and movement prior to unpacking. The packaging techniques may vary according to customer geographical location and mode of transportation. The most common will be described in this section and others will be self-explanatory using this section as a guideline. The grinder is in two crates. The control pedestal for the computer control is unattached and packed in a smaller crate with the coolant system. The remainder of the grinder is in a larger crate. Carefully move the unit to its permanent location, remove from pallet and inspect for shipping damage. If damage is evident notify the transportation carrier. See Technical Characteristics of the Machine for further information on the shipping crates. Contact Pride Engineering for additional information on unpacking and initial set-up of the grinding machine.

Installation: 

Remove the grinder assembly from its shipping pallet and position it on a clean, level floor. See the Grinder footprint for floor layout and connection points.

Use only lift points indicated on the machine with lift point labels.

Only qualified personnel should connect the electrical power to the grinder.

Connect 380-480 volt, 3 phase, 50/60 hz electrical service to the rear right side of the machine. Please refer to the electrical schematic. The equipment uses a multi-tap transformer that may need to be changed for your input voltage. All internal electrical hookups should be completed before the grinder is energized with the plantâ€™s electrical service.

 

  1. Connect the air supply to the system on the left side of the cabinet. You must provide a minimum of 80psi(5.6bar) to the system at all times.

    Never spin or operate the air bearing spindles without compressed air pressure supplied to the air bearings.

  2. Open the cabinet front doors and you will see the air controls. Pressurize the granite plate air mount (4 places) to 25-30 psi (1.7 - 2.1 bar) suspending the granite plate on the air bags.

  3.  

  4. Turn on the air supply. You will notice the granite table lift when the air pressure reaches an adequate level.

  5. After energizing the air mounts under the granite block, adjust the four (4) leveling feet located under the grinder near the lift points. Each foot will be underneath a corner of the granite block. Use a precision level resting on the granite block. Level the granite side to side, front to back and corner to corner. Ignore the two (2) feet on the right end under the controls until the four leveling pads supporting the granite plate are level. After the granite is level, adjust the feet under the electrical control. Ensure that the granite leveling did not change after adjustment.

  6. Fill coolant reservoir with the coolant provided. Use ratio of 20 parts water to 1 part coolant. The coolant tank holds approximately 21 gallons (80 Liters). Therefore, you should add 128 ounces (3.8 liters) of coolant and fill the rest of the tank with water to achieve the 20:1 ratio of water to coolant. Use only Pride approved coolant!
